Projects

My Roommate Collaboration App

I worked in a team of five to build a webapp that allowed users to create a household with their roommates in which they could schedule and manage chores. A core element of the app was the gamification of these activities, we rewarded chore completion with XP that allowed users to flex their ranking.

The app was full stack using Python django, HTML/CSS, SQLite, and javascript with AJAX. There is core functionality, like login/registration, task/event creation and rendering, and account/household management. We created a Kanban board where users drag and drop their chores into various levels of completion.

I worked as the scrum master, which placed a lot of individual responsibilty on me. I helped my teammates frequently when they would run into issues with the stack, I helped set sprint velocity, and I managed the GitHub repo. I enjoyed the responsibility this role gave me, as I learned communication, pair programming, and merging and manage pull requests.

Sepsis Prediction

I worked on a team of two to predict onset of sepsis at and before the time of incidence from ICU data.

Our data was sparse time-seriesdata in places, so we used a combination of forward fill, backward fill, and linear interpolation to impute our data. We trained Logistic Regression, K-nearest neighbors, Random Forest, and XGBoost. We achieved our best results with XGB with an AUROC of 0.866, which is similar to some state of the art algorithms.

This project was super fun and interesting because it exposed us to a real world problem. We also had the opportunity to work with a diverse set of libraries, namely: pandas, numpy, sklearn, pytorch, and XGBoost.
